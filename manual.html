Third Eye for the Blind

Working Manual

Introduction

The Third Eye device is designed to enhance the mobility and independence of visually impaired individuals. By leveraging advanced sensor technology, it provides real-time feedback about obstacles in the user’s environment, enabling safer navigation.

Components

  • Ultrasonic Sensors: Detect obstacles by emitting sound waves and measuring the echo.
  • Microcontroller: Processes data from the sensors and triggers audible alerts.
  • Speaker/Buzzer: Provides audible alerts to the user.
  • Battery: Powers the device for portability.
  • Mounting Frame: Attaches the device to existing mobility aids.

Setup Instructions

  1. Unbox the Third Eye device and ensure all components are present.
  2. Attach the device to your mobility aid using the provided mounting frame.
  3. Turn on the device using the power switch located on the side.
  4. Ensure the sensors are unobstructed and facing forward for optimal detection.

How It Works

The Third Eye device uses ultrasonic sensors to detect objects in the user’s path. When an obstacle is detected:

  • The sensors measure the distance to the obstacle.
  • The microcontroller processes the data and triggers an audible alert if the obstacle is within a predefined range.
  • The alert helps the user navigate safely by identifying the location of obstacles.

Maintenance

  • Regularly clean the sensors with a soft, dry cloth to ensure accuracy.
  • Check the battery level and recharge as needed.
  • Inspect the mounting frame for wear and tear, and replace if necessary.

Safety Guidelines

  • Do not expose the device to extreme temperatures or moisture.
  • Ensure the sensors are not blocked by any objects during use.
  • Turn off the device when not in use to conserve battery life.
